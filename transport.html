🚀 Singapore Transport Guide 2025: Move Like a Local

From Changi Airport to your hotel, this is how **real Singaporeans get around** — fast, cheap, and without breaking a sweat.

🛬 Just Landed at Changi Airport?

You’ve arrived at one of the world’s best airports. Here’s what to do first:

  • Get a SIM Card: Head to the Singtel, StarHub, or M1 counters in T1, T2, T3. Or buy an eSIM via Klook before landing for instant activation.
  • Grab Your Luggage & Exit: Follow signs to baggage claim and immigration.
  • Choose Your Ride:
    • MRT: Take the East-West Line from T2/T3 to City Hall (~25 mins, $2.50).
    • Taxi: ~$30–$40 to downtown. Use Grab for fixed pricing.
    • Bus: Cheaper but slower. Bus 36 to Tanah Merah MRT.
  • Explore Jewel Changi: Don’t miss the Rain Vortex (free), Canopy Park (paid), and Butterfly Garden (paid).

🚇 MRT (Mass Rapid Transit)

The backbone of Singapore’s transport. Fast, clean, and connects everywhere.

  • Lines: North-South, East-West, Circle, Downtown, Thomson-East Coast
  • Fare: $0.80 – $3.00 (distance-based)
  • Operating Hours: 5:30 AM – 12:30 AM (Fri/Sat: up to 1:30 AM)
  • Top Tip: Use TransitLink app for real-time schedules.
💡 Cheat: Tap in AND out — or you’ll be charged the max fare!

🚌 Bus

Great for last-mile connections and scenic routes. Buses go where MRT doesn’t.

  • Operators: SBS Transit, SMRT Buses, Tower Transit, Go-Ahead
  • Fare: $0.92 – $2.50 (distance-based)
  • Free Transfers: Switch from MRT to bus (or vice versa) within 45 mins.
  • Apps: SBS Transit, MyTransport.SG, Google Maps
💡 Cheat: Senior citizens & students get 50% off with Concession Card.

🚕 Taxi & Ride-Hailing

Comfortable, but pricier. Best for late nights or group travel.

  • Providers: Grab, Gojek, ComfortDelGro, CDG Premier, Strides
  • Fare: $3.50 base + $0.22–$0.33/km. Grab app shows estimated fare.
  • Tipping: Not expected, but rounding up is common.
  • Surge Pricing: High during rain, peak hours, or events.
💡 Cheat: Book via Grab — often cheaper than hailing on the street.

🚗 Self-Drive & Car Rentals

Not common for tourists, but possible if you want flexibility.

  • Rental Companies: Avis, Hertz, Klook Car Rental
  • License: International Driving Permit (IDP) required if your license isn’t in English.
  • ERP (Electronic Road Pricing): Tolls apply during peak hours. Rental cars include an In-Vehicle Unit (IU).
  • Parking: Use Parking.sg for on-street parking.
💡 Cheat: Avoid driving in the CBD — parking is expensive and traffic is heavy.

🚲 Cycling

Singapore is bike-friendly! Rentals at MRT stations, bus stops, and parks.

  • Cycling Paths: Park Connector Network (PCN) spans 300+ km.
  • Rental Apps:
    • Anywheel: Bikes at MRT stations and bus stops.
    • SG Bike: E-bikes and regular bikes.
    • oBike: City-wide docking stations.
  • Rental Cost: $1–$3/hour. Pay via app.
  • Rules: No cycling on sidewalks. Helmets recommended.
💡 Cheat: Use Anywheel at bus stops — super convenient for last-mile travel.

🚶 Walking

Many areas are walkable, especially in the city and parks.

  • Walkable Zones: Marina Bay, Clarke Quay, East Coast Park, Sentosa, Tiong Bahru
  • Underground Walkways: Connected from Raffles Place to City Hall to Marina Bay.
  • Sheltered Paths: Most sidewalks have covered walkways — perfect in rain.
  • Distance: Most MRT stations are 5–10 mins apart on foot.
💡 Cheat: Wear light clothes — it’s hot and humid year-round.

🚌 Group Tours & Guided Transport

See Singapore with experts — great for first-timers.

  • Hop-On Hop-Off Bus: Open-top double-decker. Covers Marina Bay, Sentosa, Orchard.
  • Guided Walking Tours: Chinatown, Little India, Kampong Glam — learn history & food culture.
  • Private Tours: Custom itineraries via Klook or Pelago.
  • River Cruises: 40-min bumboat ride from Boat Quay to Marina Bay.
💡 Cheat: Book combo tours (e.g., HOHO + Sentosa) for 20% off.

📱 Essential Tools & Apps

These will save your trip.

  • TransitLink App: Real-time MRT/bus schedules, fare calculator.
  • Google Maps: Still the best for walking & transit directions.
  • Grab: Ride-hailing, food delivery, and even motorbike taxis.
  • MyTransport.SG: Official app for ERP, parking, and public transport.
  • Citymapper: Alternative to Google Maps with better transit focus.
💡 Cheat: Download offline maps — saves data and works without signal.

🔥 Pro Tips for Smart Travel

  • ✅ Tap in AND tap out — or pay max fare.
  • ✅ Free transfers between MRT and bus within 45 mins.
  • ✅ Use EZ-Link or NETS FlashPay — buy at MRT stations or 7-Eleven.
  • ✅ Top up online via TransitLink app or at any machine.
  • ✅ Tourist Pass: Unlimited rides for 1/2/3 days — great for heavy users.
  • ✅ Buy a tourist SIM at Changi Airport (Singtel, StarHub, M1) or pre-book an eSIM via Klook.
  • ✅ Grab is cheaper than taxis — especially for short trips.
  • ✅ Walk between City Hall and Raffles Place — only 10 mins, no need to MRT.

Final Tip: Mix transport modes. MRT to the city, walk to the hawker centre, Grab back to hotel. That’s how locals do it.
