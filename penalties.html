🚨 Singapore Fines & Penalties 2025: What’s Real, What’s Myth

Look, I get it — you’ve heard the stories: “Chewing gum = jail.” “Jaywalking = deportation.” “Spitting = instant fine.”

After 4 years here, I can tell you: some are true, some are exaggerations.

Yes, Singapore has rules. But it’s not a police state. Locals follow them too — not because they’re scared, but because it keeps the city clean, safe, and running smoothly.

This guide? It’s not to scare you. It’s to help you respect the culture — so you can enjoy your trip without any “Wah lau, I got fined” moments.

📋 Common Fines in Singapore (2025)

Yes, these apply to tourists too. No exceptions.

Offense Typical Fine Reality Check
🚭 Smoking in prohibited areas (MRT, parks, hawker centres) $500–$1,000 Enforced heavily. Look for signs — no smoking zones are everywhere.
🚭 Vaping or using e-cigarettes $5,000–$10,000 or jail 100% illegal. No exceptions. Even bringing it in can get you fined.
🚯 Littering (including food wrappers, cigarette butts) $300–$5,000 Enforced daily. NEA officers patrol hawker centres and parks.
🚶‍♂️ Jaywalking (crossing outside pedestrian crossings) $150 Yes, it’s enforced. Use the zebra crossing — it’s not hard.
🚮 Feeding pigeons or monkeys $400 Common in parks like Botanic Gardens. Officers are strict.
🚭 Chewing gum (sale & import banned) $100–$1,000 You won’t go to jail for chewing it, but don’t buy or sell it.
🚭 Spitting in public $1,000 Rare, but possible. Just don’t do it.
🚭 Drinking alcohol in public after 10:30 PM (Liquor Control Zones) $1,000 Applies to areas like Orchard, Clarke Quay, Little India. Check signs.
🚇 Eating or drinking on MRT/LRT (except water) $500 Enforced by plainclothes officers. Even a sip of coffee can get you caught.
🚭 Urinating in public (e.g., alleys, stairwells) $3,000 Yes, people do this. Yes, they get fined.
🚭 Possession of drugs (even small amounts) Fines + jail + caning Don’t even think about it. Chope your life, not drugs.
🚫 Releasing sky lanterns or drones without permit $20,000 Even small drones need approval from CAAS.
🚫 Vandalism (e.g., graffiti, keying cars) $2,000 + jail + caning Famous case: Michael Fay. Don’t test it.
🚫 Bringing in fake goods (e.g., counterfeit bags, watches) $10,000 or jail Even if you bought it elsewhere, importing fake goods is illegal.
🚫 Using mobile phone at pedestrian crossings $150 New rule. Officers patrol busy crossings like Orchard Road.

💡 Bro, Here’s My Real Talk Rule Hacks

  • Download the NEA app: See real-time fines and alerts.
  • Carry a small bag: For tissues until you find a bin — saves you from littering fines.
  • Use pedestrian crossings: Even if no cars are coming — it’s the law.
  • Don’t vape: It’s not worth the risk. 100% illegal.
  • Water is allowed on MRT: But no other drinks or food.
  • Alcohol after 10:30 PM? Only in licensed venues (bars, restaurants).
  • Chewing gum? You won’t be jailed, but don’t buy it here.

Remember: Locals follow these rules too. It’s not about fear — it’s about respect.
